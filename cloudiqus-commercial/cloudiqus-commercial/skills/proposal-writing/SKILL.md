# Proposal Writing Skill

## Proposal Structure (max 8 pages)
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page) — their problem, our solution, the outcome, the investment
2. **Understanding Your Challenge** (1 page) — show you listened, reflect their pain back
3. **Our Approach** (2 pages) — phases, methodology, what we do and when
4. **Why CloudiQS** (1 page) — differentiators, proof points, team
5. **Investment** (1 page) — pricing, payment milestones, AWS funding offset
6. **Next Steps** (half page) — one clear action, one date

## Writing Rules
- Lead with their problem, not our credentials
- Every claim must be specific — never "we're experts in AWS"
- Use their language — mirror the words they used in the discovery call
- Show the funding offset prominently — it dramatically reduces perceived cost
- End with ONE clear next step and a specific date

## Proof Point Selection
Match proof points to their sector and pain:
- Education customer → Warwick University, Education4Fun.ai, OCRE
- VMware migration → cite specific VM count, timeline, funding achieved
- FinOps → specific £/month saving, specific percentage
- Agent Bakery → working agent, specific use case, specific outcome

## What Kills Proposals
- Generic opening ("CloudiQS is an AWS Advanced Partner...")
- No quantification of their pain
- Too long — stop at 8 pages
- Vague deliverables ("guidance and recommendations")
- No AWS funding mention
- Weak CTA ("please let us know if you have questions")
