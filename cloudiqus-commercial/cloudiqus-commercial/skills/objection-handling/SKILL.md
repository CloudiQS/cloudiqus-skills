# Objection Handling Skill

## Pricing Objections
**"You're too expensive"**
→ "Compared to what? If we find £5K/month in savings, our engagement pays for itself in 3 months. And AWS MAP often funds part of the migration cost."

**"We don't have budget"**
→ "That's exactly why we start with a funded assessment. WAFR credits can cover the cost of a full architecture review — you pay nothing until we've proven the value."

## Timing Objections
**"Not the right time"**
→ "When does your VMware contract renew? If it's in the next 12 months, now is exactly the right time to start planning."

**"We're in a freeze"**
→ "Understood. Can we use this time to do the assessment so you're ready to move when the freeze lifts? The WAFR review is low-cost and high-value."

## Incumbent Objections
**"We have an existing AWS partner"**
→ "We work alongside existing partners all the time. Is there a specific area they're not covering — FinOps, AI, security — where a second opinion would add value?"

**"We manage AWS ourselves"**
→ "Most teams do. Our value isn't replacing your team — it's giving them 18 specialist engineers on call, and funding programmes your team doesn't have access to."

## Risk Objections
**"Migration is too risky"**
→ "We always start with a pilot — 1–3 workloads, no commitment to the full migration until it's proven. You control the pace."

**"We've had bad experiences with consultants"**
→ "Tell me what went wrong. We're structured differently — fixed price, named engineers, and we don't leave until it works."

## AI Scepticism
**"AI is just hype"**
→ "We're building production agents, not demos. The FinOps agent we built for [customer] saves them 3 hours per month per account — I can show you the code."

**"We tried AI and it didn't work"**
→ "What were you trying to do? Most failed AI projects weren't deployed on the right infrastructure. Bedrock AgentCore changes that significantly."
