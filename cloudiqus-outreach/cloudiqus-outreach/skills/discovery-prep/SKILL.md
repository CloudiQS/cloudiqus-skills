# Discovery Prep Skill

## Research (before every call)
- Company basics, tech stack signals, recent news
- Who is on the call — title, tenure, LinkedIn
- Their current cloud provider
- Any prior CloudiQS touchpoints

## Pre-Call Hypothesis (write before the call)
- **Most likely pain:** "They are probably struggling with X because Y"
- **Best hook:** VMware / Cost / Security / AI — and why
- **Likely funding:** MAP / POC / WAFR — and why
- **Decision maker:** Who holds the budget

## Call Structure (45 minutes)
| Min | What |
|-----|------|
| 0–5 | Rapport, agenda, roles confirmed |
| 5–15 | Their world: "Tell me about your current AWS setup" |
| 15–30 | PAIN: Problem → Authority → Impact → Need/Timeline |
| 30–40 | CloudiQS: relevant proof point, funding angle |
| 40–45 | Next steps: specific date, specific action |

## Proof Points Bank
- "Fastest Growing AWS Advanced Partner of the Year (UK)"
- "We've helped [sector] companies save 20–40% on AWS spend"
- "We migrated [X] workloads off VMware in [Y] weeks, funded by AWS MAP"
- "We built a production AI agent for [sector] on AWS Bedrock in 6 weeks"
- "We ran a WAFR and helped the customer claim £[X] in AWS credits"

## Output Format
One-page call brief:
1. Company snapshot (5 bullets)
2. Pre-call hypothesis (pain / hook / funding / decision maker)
3. 5 tailored PAIN questions
4. Best proof point to use
5. Proposed next step to close the call with
