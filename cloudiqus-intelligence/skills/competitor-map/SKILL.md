# Competitor Map Skill

## Direct Competitors (AWS partners, UK)

### Tier 1 — Large System Integrators
**Accenture, Capgemini, Deloitte**
- Strength: brand, scale, global
- Weakness: expensive, slow, not SMB-focused, no agility
- CloudiQS response: "We're not a £5K/day big-4 engagement. We're 18 specialists who actually build and run it."

### Tier 2 — Mid-Size AWS Partners
**Logicalis, Atos, SCC, Softcat**
- Strength: established, hardware relationships, broad portfolio
- Weakness: AWS is one of many vendors, not specialist, slow to adopt new services
- CloudiQS response: "We're AWS-only. That means we know the funding programmes, the competencies, and the latest services better than anyone."

### Tier 3 — Boutique AWS Partners
Various small shops
- Strength: low cost, flexible
- Weakness: limited scale, no funding expertise, narrow skills
- CloudiQS response: "We have 18 engineers and ex-AWS team members. We can staff the full project without going offshore."

## When a Competitor Is Mentioned
1. Never criticise them directly
2. Ask: "What are they proposing?" (understand their angle)
3. Ask: "What would success look like for you?" (refocus on outcomes)
4. Differentiate on: AWS funding expertise, ex-AWS team, Agent Bakery, Education competency

## CloudiQS Unique Differentiators (no one else has all of these)
1. Ex-AWS engineers on the team
2. Agent Bakery — production AI agents on Bedrock AgentCore
3. AWS Activate Provider status
4. Education sector competency + OCRE subcontractor
5. Fastest Growing AWS Advanced Partner of the Year (UK)
